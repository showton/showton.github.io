WAITING ROOM GAME DESIGN

Waiting Room is a guerrilla board game designed for an Impact Games pitch presentation


PROJECT INTENT

Waiting Room is meant to be played in the actual waiting room of a college health center. Divided into three separate themes--Everyday Health, Mental Health, and Women's Health--the game focuses on the challenge to receive good healthcare as a college student. The design of the game itself is on a three sided brochure with the rules on one third, punch-out scenario and move cards on the second, and the board itself on the third. Students can play discreetly and pass it on to other students easily with this design.


WOMEN'S HEALTH

This mockup focuses on the issue of women's health at the Engemmann Student Health Center, specifically the issues of obtaining birth control, getting treatment for a urinary tract infection, and sexual assault.


THE BOARD

The structure of the game is similar to Clue and Chutes and Ladders. Each player gets a scenario card describing their ailment. Players play the nose game to determine who starts (which mimics the randomness of receiving treatment at the health center). Players take turns drawing move cards which give players either advantages or disadvantages. This mockup works with the Women's Health theme.
