Designing Shoot the Book! 2017 + 2018

VISUAL DESIGN

PROFESSIONAL WORK

Overview.

Duration: August - November, 2017 and 2018

Tools + skills: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign, Adobe Photoshop, print design, branding, graphic illustration, layout design

About the program: Shoot the Book! is an annual program held across the world and hosted by the French General Consulate of Los Angeles that pitches French short stories, novels, and graphic novels to Los Angeles filmmakers. Each page has a plot summary, author information, cover art for the book, and indicators whether or not it’s a jury recommendation. Each year I was tasked with creating:

  • the overall branding of the year
  • a 28-32 page booklet
  • a 33 x 81 inch kakemono
  • a 8 x 8 foot step-and-repeat
  • a Variety magazine half-page advertisement
  • 2 save the dates and 2 invitations to the event
  • and a press release design

Shoot the Book! 2017.

For 2017’s booklet, I wanted to pay homage to books and the Abstract Expressionist movement, particularly Wassily Kandinsky. The cover plays with the general form of a book, but abstracts the shape and layers different shapes on top of each other using various opacities. The bright colors are inspired from the 2017 Cannes booklet.

Shoot the Book! 2018.

For the 2018 booklet, I wanted to use a modern art deco theme with its bright colors and bold, geometric shapes. I wanted to evoke a summertime, swimming feel and chose “pool colors” as a result, shaping the books to almost look like umbrellas. This gave the booklet a very “LA” vibe.
